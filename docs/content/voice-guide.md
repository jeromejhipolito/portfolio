# Voice & Tone Reference

## The Voice IS:
1. **Precise** — specific numbers, concrete claims, no vague adjectives
2. **Confident** — states what was built and how, doesn't hedge or apologize
3. **Restrained** — lets the work speak; one strong sentence beats three qualifiers

## The Voice is NOT:
1. **Boastful** — no "rockstar", "ninja", "10x", no superlatives without proof
2. **Casual** — no slang, no emojis in copy, no "basically" or "just"
3. **Corporate** — no "leveraging synergies", no buzzword chains, no third-person self-reference

## Example Sentences (Correct Tone)
> "The booking system processes 200+ concurrent reservations without data conflicts. Saga pattern handles the multi-step transaction; idempotency keys prevent duplicate charges on retry."

> "I write 500+ automated tests not because someone asked me to, but because I deploy with confidence."

## Example Sentences (Wrong Tone — Avoid)
> ~~"I'm a passionate full-stack developer with extensive experience building cutting-edge solutions."~~
> ~~"Leveraging state-of-the-art microservices architecture to deliver scalable, robust, enterprise-grade platforms."~~

## Rules
- **Grade 10 reading level** (verify with Hemingway App if unsure)
- **Every technical term gets a plain-language pair** (BR-027)
  - Right: "Saga pattern — managing multi-step transactions across services with automatic rollback on failure"
  - Wrong: "Implements Saga choreography with compensating transactions"
- **Numbers over adjectives**: "500+ tests" not "comprehensive testing", "6 years" not "extensive experience"
- **Active voice**: "I built" not "was responsible for building"
- **One idea per sentence**: If a sentence has "and" twice, split it
